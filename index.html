Dry hire vs wet hire

What's dry hire?

Dry hire — what we do

You hire the machine only and operate it yourself. It's the cheapest way to get earthmoving done if you (or someone on your crew) can operate — typically less than half the cost of wet hire. Perfect for owner-builders, landscapers, fencing contractors and small operators around the Hills.

Wet hire — machine + operator

Wet hire includes an operator and is usually charged by the hour with minimum call-outs. We can quote wet hire for bigger jobs on request — email us with your site and scope. But if you can drive it yourself, dry hire will save you serious money.

FAQ

Straight answers

What licence do I need for the tipper truck?
A Medium Rigid (MR) licence or higher. The Hino FD 500 has a GVM over 8 tonnes, so a car licence isn't enough. You'll upload your licence when booking and show it at pickup — no MR, no keys, no exceptions (it keeps our insurance valid and you legal).
Do I need a ticket for the excavator in SA?
No high-risk work licence exists for excavators anymore — the old LE class was phased out nationally in 2013. But you must be genuinely competent, and we require a signed competency declaration (experience, RIIMPO320-style training, or a VOC). On construction sites you'll also need a white card. First-timers: book wet hire instead — an inexperienced operator can do expensive damage fast.
What exactly is dry hire?
Machine only, no operator — you drive it. That's why the rate is a fraction of wet hire. Fuel: bring it back full (diesel only), or we refuel at $3.30/L plus a $25 service fee.
What's your cancellation policy?
Cancel 7+ days before pickup: full refund of your deposit. Between 2 and 7 days: 50% refund. Inside 48 hours: the deposit is forfeited (the machine was held for you and turned others away). Weather call? Ring us — we're reasonable about genuine washouts and will usually move your booking free.
How does yard-to-yard timing work?
Your hire starts when the gear leaves our Crafers yard and ends when it's back. A half day is up to 4 hours; a full day is one calendar day. Running late? Call us — late returns without notice are charged at the half-day rate.
What's the bond and when do I get it back?
$500 (excavator) or $750 (truck) pre-authorised on your card at pickup — not charged unless there's damage, missing gear, or cleaning/refuelling needed. Released within 2 business days of a clean return.
What if I damage the machine?
Under dry-hire terms you're responsible for the equipment while it's in your care. An optional damage waiver (added at pickup) caps your exposure to an excess instead of full value — but it's void for the obvious sins: overloading, water immersion, operating under the influence, or letting an unlicensed/incompetent person operate. Full details in our hire terms.
Can you deliver?
Yes — Hills and southern suburbs delivery by arrangement. Put your site suburb in the booking notes and we'll confirm a delivery fee before you pay anything extra. Yard pickup at Crafers is always free.
Why book with Heads & Tails instead of the big hire chains?
Three reasons: we're closer (Crafers, not the city or Lonsdale), you book online in two minutes against a live calendar instead of phone tag, and our owner-operator rates undercut the big-brand yards. Local gear, looked after properly, from people who actually use it.
