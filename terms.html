2. The hire

2.1 We agree to hire the Equipment to you for the Hire Period on these Terms. This Agreement is formed when you accept these Terms online or sign the Hire Schedule, and pay the Deposit.

2.2 The Equipment remains our property at all times. You have possession as a bailee only (see clauses 17 and 18).

2.3 We may decline any booking, or terminate at pickup, if in our reasonable opinion you cannot demonstrate the licences or competency required by clause 8, or you appear affected by alcohol or drugs.

3. Hire Period — yard-to-yard

3.1 All hires are yard-to-yard: the Hire Period starts at Commencement and ends only when the Equipment is returned to our yard at Crafers SA and accepted by us (or, where we have agreed to collect it, when we collect it). Time spent transporting the Equipment is part of the Hire Period.

3.2 Hire periods are:

  • (a) Half day — up to 4 hours yard-to-yard, returned by the time on the Hire Schedule;
  • (b) Full day — up to 24 hours yard-to-yard (or such shorter same-day window shown on the Hire Schedule), with engine/operating hours limited as per clause 3.4;
  • (c) Weekly — 7 consecutive days yard-to-yard.

3.3 Late return. If the Equipment is not returned by the end of the Hire Period:

  • (a) additional Hire Charges accrue at the applicable half-day or daily rate (a return more than 1 hour late converts a half-day hire to a full-day hire; each further 24 hours or part thereof is charged at the full daily rate); and
  • (b) if the lateness causes us to compensate or turn away another customer, you must also pay our reasonable resulting costs.

3.4 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, daily hire assumes up to 8 machine/engine hours per 24 hours. Excess hours are charged pro-rata at 1/8th of the daily rate per hour, read from the hour meter.

3.5 Extensions are only effective if requested before the end of the Hire Period and confirmed by us in writing; extended periods are charged at the then-current rates.

3.6 If you finish early, no refund is payable for the unused portion of the Hire Period unless we agree otherwise in writing.

4.1 Hire Charges are as published or quoted at the time of booking and shown on the Hire Schedule. Unless expressly stated otherwise, published hire rates are inclusive of GST; other amounts payable under this Agreement (such as charges for damage, cleaning or recovery) are exclusive of GST, which will be added at the prevailing rate.

4.2 Hire Charges do not include fuel, delivery/float charges, cleaning beyond a basic wash-down, damage, or any pass-through amounts under this Agreement.

4.3 We may charge a card surcharge that reflects our reasonable cost of acceptance.

6. Security bond

6.1 A Bond is payable before Commencement, by pre-authorisation hold on your credit/debit card (or bank transfer if agreed). The standard Bond is $500 per item of Equipment ($750 for a combined Truck + Excavator hire), or such other amount shown on the Hire Schedule. We may set a higher Bond for high-risk hires at our discretion, advised before booking is confirmed.

6.2 We may apply the Bond toward any amount you owe under this Agreement, including damage up to the applicable excess, fuel, cleaning, late-return charges, fines and recovery costs.

6.3 The Bond (or its balance) will be released within 5 business days of return of the Equipment in accordance with this Agreement, once inspection is complete. If a claim is unresolved (for example, a repair quote is pending), we may retain the Bond, or the disputed portion, until the amount is determined, acting reasonably and keeping you informed.

You (or your agent) must inspect the Equipment at pickup and note any pre-existing damage on the condition report; the report is otherwise taken to be accurate.

If you transport the Excavator, you must use a vehicle and trailer/float with adequate rated capacity, comply with all load restraint and mass requirements (including the current Load Restraint Guide), and check couplings, chains and lights before travel. You are fully responsible for the Equipment during transport.

8. Hirer's obligations — operators and licensing

8.1 You must ensure the Equipment is operated only by persons who are:

  • (a) for the Truck — the holder of a current Australian MR (Medium Rigid) or higher class driver's licence, whose licence details we have sighted and recorded before Commencement;
  • (b) for the Excavator — trained, competent and experienced in the operation of hydraulic excavators and each attachment used, and aged 18 or over. Before Commencement the operator must complete and sign our Operator Competency Declaration describing their training/experience (and provide any relevant tickets, e.g. statements of attainment or verification of competency, if held);
  • (c) named or approved by us in writing — the Equipment must not be operated by anyone else; and
  • (d) not under the influence of alcohol or any drug (including prescription medication that impairs operation) while operating, driving or transporting the Equipment.

8.2 You must ensure every operator, before use, reads and complies with the operator's manual, safety decals and any instructions we provide, and wears appropriate PPE.

8.3 We may refuse to release, or may repossess, the Equipment if these requirements are not met, without refund of Hire Charges for time lost.

9. Hirer's obligations — site safety and use

9.1 Before You Dig. Before any excavation, drilling or ground-breaking, you must obtain and comply with current Before You Dig Australia (BYDA) plans (byda.com.au, formerly Dial Before You Dig) for the Site, locate all underground services, and comply with any asset-owner permit conditions. You are solely liable for damage to underground or overhead services and all consequential claims.

9.2 You must comply with all applicable laws, including the Work Health and Safety Act 2012 (SA) and equivalent legislation, road traffic laws, and local council requirements; conduct your own site risk assessment; maintain safe exclusion zones (including from overhead powerlines); and keep children and unauthorised persons away from the Equipment at all times.

9.3 Daily care. Each day of use you must, at your cost:

  • (a) carry out prestart checks (engine oil, coolant, hydraulic oil, leaks, tracks/tyres, lights, horn, seatbelt, attachment pins);
  • (b) grease all lubrication points on the Excavator and attachments in accordance with the manual (including breaker tool greasing at the intervals specified — typically every 2 hours of breaker use);
  • (c) check and maintain correct track tension on the Excavator;
  • (d) stop use and notify us immediately if any warning light, unusual noise, leak or fault appears (see clause 16).

9.4 Fuel. The Equipment must be fuelled with diesel only, from a reputable retail supply. You are responsible for all damage caused by incorrect, contaminated or insufficient fuel (including running dry) or incorrect fluids/AdBlue. The Equipment must be returned with a full tank; otherwise refuelling is charged at $3.30 per litre plus a $25 service fee.

9.5 Cleaning. The Equipment must be returned clean of mud, concrete, rubbish and loose material. Excessive cleaning is charged at $90/hr + GST. Loads carried in the Truck must be lawful, non-hazardous and fully removed before return.

9.6 Security. When unattended, the Equipment must be locked, keys removed and stored securely, and where reasonably possible kept in a locked yard, shed or fenced site. You bear the risk of theft (clause 11).

10. Prohibited uses

10.1 The Equipment must not be:

  • (a) used where asbestos (including friable asbestos) is or may be present, or for demolition of structures that may contain asbestos, without our prior written consent and full compliance with asbestos-removal law;
  • (b) used in, on or over water, in tidal zones, riverbeds, dams or waterways, or on wharves, barges or vessels; or immersed (fully or partly) in water or mud beyond normal ground conditions;
  • (c) overloaded — including exceeding the Truck's rated payload (~5.5 t) or GVM/axle limits, or the Excavator's rated lift/attachment capacities;
  • (d) used underground or in mines or tunnels;
  • (e) taken outside South Australia without our prior written consent;
  • (f) used to tow anything (other than a trailer within the Truck's rated towing capacity, if we consent in writing);
  • (g) used for any illegal purpose, in contravention of any law, or on any race, trial or test;
  • (h) sub-hired, lent, or given into the possession of any third party;
  • (i) modified, repaired or tampered with (including disabling telematics/GPS or safety devices), or fitted with attachments not supplied or approved by us; or
  • (j) used with the rock breaker for lifting, prying or levering (breaker is for breaking only, at correct angles per the manual).

10.2 Identification plates, decals and safety signage must not be removed or defaced.

11.4 You must report any theft or suspected criminal damage to the police within 24 hours and give us the police report number, and provide any statements, photos or statutory declarations we reasonably require.

  • (c) the cost of loss or damage to tyres, tracks, glass, hoses, teeth, pins, keys, and accessories/attachments (buckets, auger, breaker tools), which are not covered by the waiver.

  • (b) overloading or exceeding rated capacities;
  • (c) use in, on, over or under water, or full or partial immersion;
  • (d) any prohibited use under clause 10 or other breach of this Agreement that caused or increased the risk of the loss or damage;
  • (e) gross negligence, recklessness, misuse, abuse or wilful or malicious acts;
  • (f) failure to carry out the daily care obligations in clause 9.3 (including lack of lubrication or running without oil/coolant/track tension);
  • (g) theft or unexplained disappearance where the Equipment was not secured in accordance with clause 9.6, or where no police report is provided within 7 days;
